Revisiting wet-lab processes from the basics About our pursuit for efficient bacterial growth

Traditional bacterial cultures require consistent monitoring of optical densities over an entire day (sometimes even longer), to determine an optimal point for injecting the reagent. This process is often the initial step to a much longer experimental process, yet takes significant time in itself. It is estimated that an average lab technician wastes nearly 1 hour in recording optical densities during culturing!

We saw an opportunity in significantly reducing the labour cost/time in creating these cultures, by automating the spectrophotometry. Our initial market research has shown significant interest in a modular automated process, which not only has advantages in terms of labour, but can reduce waste as well, by reducing manual lab work.
